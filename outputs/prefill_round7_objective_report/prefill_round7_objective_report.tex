\section*{Question}
Round 6 ruled out static boundary-summary fusion as the main next lever: under a cleaner train-side holdout selector, the all-layer last-token \textbf{anchor} was again the best prefill arm. The next compact question was therefore training-side: can the same anchor be improved by either (1) selector alignment to within-source$\times$length-bin PR-AUC or (2) a small length-robust objective such as equal-bin BCE or within-bin ranking?

\section*{Setup}
I kept the feature view and datasets fixed and changed only training / selection.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Data}: unchanged from Rounds 4--6 (source-control train, natural test, source/length-matched test), using the saved Round 4 anchor tensors.
  \item \textbf{Metadata branch}: same validated legacy control.
  \item \textbf{Base selector}: holdout \texttt{PR-AUC} on a fixed 15\% train-side split, with holdout within-bin PR-AUC as tie-break.
  \item \textbf{Free selector-only diagnostic}: reselect the already-trained Round 6 anchor by holdout within-bin PR-AUC without retraining.
  \item \textbf{Round 7 batch}: three 3-seed anchor-only arms:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item large-batch BCE control,
      \item equal-bin BCE,
      \item equal-bin BCE + within-bin pairwise ranking.
    \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

The equal-bin groups were source$\times$token-length terciles on the train split. The ranking term used logistic pairwise loss on the final metadata-adjusted logit.

\begin{itemize}

\section*{Interpretation}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Selector alignment is not the missing trick.} The no-retrain diagnostic on the saved Round 6 anchor already shows that selecting by holdout within-bin PR-AUC pushes the checkpoint in the wrong direction on both matched PR-AUC and matched within-bin PR-AUC.
  \item \textbf{The best prefill arm is still the original Round 6 anchor.} Even the large-batch BCE control, which keeps the same feature view and stable selector while changing only the optimization regime, loses about \textbf{0.012} matched PR-AUC and \textbf{0.015} matched within-bin PR-AUC.
  \item \textbf{Equal-bin BCE overcorrects.} It is materially worse than both the Round 6 anchor and the large-batch BCE control on the matched eval.
  \item \textbf{The ranking auxiliary only partially repairs that damage.} It improves on equal-bin BCE, but it still does not beat the simpler large-batch BCE control, and it stays clearly below the Round 6 anchor.
  \item \textbf{The scientific conclusion is now sharper.} Within the current one-bit target + static-anchor setup, both boundary fusion and small length-robust objective tweaks appear exhausted. The remaining prefill signal is real above metadata, but not easily unlocked by another local loss change.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Bottom Line}
Round 7 is a negative but useful result: the \textbf{original Round 6 anchor} remains the strongest prefill-only arm, and neither selector-only changes nor local grouped-objective tweaks improve on it. My read is that the next high-value round should \textbf{change the target or the representation}, not continue to tune the same one-bit static-anchor classifier. The most plausible next directions are:
\begin{itemize}
  \item onset-horizon / severity-style supervision rather than a single eventual-loop bit;
  \item an order-sensitive last-64-token prefill sequence head rather than another static summary of the final boundary state.
\end{itemize}
